The real upgrade is not asking AI to do more. It is shifting from "person who writes prompts" to "person who designs the collaboration system."
</Callout>

## Step 1: The ceiling of single-agent collaboration

Vibe Coding works well for small personal projects and short tasks. But when scope grows, changes multiply, and quality expectations rise, single-agent work begins to show limits: context congestion, amplified rework, weak review boundaries, AI slop, and cognitive debt.

### Scenario

In a small task, one assistant can stay on track. In a larger project, the same assistant starts carrying too many responsibilities: design, implementation, review, and recovery all happen in one thread. That is when the ceiling shows up.

If you look back at your M6 project, you already built shared assets like `CLAUDE.md`, Skills, MCP workflows, and a memory-bank. M7 does not replace those. It uses them as the foundation for multi-role collaboration.

## Step 2: Shift from executor to orchestrator

In Agentic Engineering, you do not start by staring at every implementation detail. You design roles, inputs, outputs, and verification boundaries first. You are managing a workflow, not just a response.

### Scenario

The difference is visible in how the work begins:
- Vibe coder: describes a request and accepts the result
- Agentic engineer: defines the system, assigns roles, and reviews the output chain

The core move is from execution to orchestration.

## Step 3: Upgrade PEV into a role-based system

You already learned Plan, Execute, Verify in M4. In M7, that loop can be distributed: a planning agent breaks down work, an execution agent implements it, and a verification agent checks it.

### Scenario

The method stays the same, but the carrier changes:
- Plan can be handled by a planning role
- Execute can be handled by a coding role
- Verify can be handled by a testing or review role

That is how a personal habit becomes a system design.

## Summary
- Single-agent collaboration has a real ceiling.
- Agentic Engineering is about role and workflow design.
- PEV still matters, but now it can be distributed across roles.
