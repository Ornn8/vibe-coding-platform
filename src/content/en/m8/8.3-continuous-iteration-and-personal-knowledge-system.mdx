What separates strong builders is often not one great project, but whether they convert each project into reusable assets for the next one.
</Callout>

## Step 1: Use feature plans to manage what comes next

Shipping is not the end of the product lifecycle. In many ways, it is the beginning. Writing separate feature plans for future work keeps iteration structured instead of turning everything into one chaotic backlog document.

### Scenario

The curriculum wants future work to stay separate from the main plan. Instead of stuffing every new idea into one growing document, each new feature gets its own plan so iteration stays readable.

## Step 2: Organize your long-term assets

By now you have accumulated prompts, Skills, templates, retrospectives, and project structures. If they stay scattered across chats and folders, their value does not compound.

### Scenario

Before:
- prompts are buried in old chats
- Skills are hard to find
- templates are copied from memory

After:
- the assets are grouped into a clear knowledge system
- the next project starts faster because the good work was preserved

<Exercise id="ex-8.3.2.1" level="L1" title="Organize your knowledge system" description="Group your prompts, Skills, templates, and retrospectives into a clear personal knowledge structure." />

## Step 3: Learn the boundaries of Vibe Coding

Mature builders neither worship AI nor reject it. They know which projects fit fast experimentation, prototypes, MVPs, and internal tools, and which domains need much stricter engineering and governance.

### Scenario

The boundary is not about whether the tool can generate something. It is about whether the risk level matches the speed advantage. Some projects are good candidates for vibe coding; others require stricter control from the start.

<Exercise id="ex-8.3.3.1" level="L3" title="Judge the boundary" description="Compare six scenarios and decide which ones fit Vibe Coding and which ones require stricter engineering control." />

## Summary
- Post-launch iteration needs independent feature plans.
- Prompts, Skills, templates, and retrospectives are knowledge assets.
- Vibe Coding has a boundary, and mature builders learn to recognize it.

## Methodology Review
This unit combines closed-loop delivery with context stabilization by turning each iteration and each lesson learned into a reusable personal system.
